MML Season Three

Season Three · Spokane, Washington · 2018

The Michigan Games

The HemoGoblins were back-to-back champions, undefeated, and widely considered unstoppable. The final season on Baldwin Avenue had other ideas — and Joel finally got his gold.

16 Teams ◆ 4 Divisions ◆ 10 Events ◆ Baldwin Ave · Spokane, WA

Named after Joel's home state of Michigan, the Michigan Games was the final tournament of the Baldwin Ave era — the third and last season played in the house on the same street where the MML was founded. Two seasons had passed. Two championships had gone to Sam. The HemoGoblins were the league's undisputed dynasty, and nothing in the preceding two years had given anyone reason to believe Season Three would be different.

It was very different.

Sin — Joel's team, a franchise that had quietly bounced between 7th and 11th in its first two seasons — put together the best campaign in the Baldwin Ave era, posting 123 points and winning the Michigan Games by two points over Jeff's Mowers. It was Joel's first championship. It was the first time Sam had not won the season. And it came at the end of the most competitive ten-event stretch the original sixteen teams had ever produced.

The Draw

Same Sixteen, Final Chapter

The sixteen original teams returned for the last time on Baldwin Avenue, sorted into four new divisional groupings. Division North drew the Mowers, Angels, Expressos, and Purps — now competing as Oysterium. Division South held the Trinitarians, Deep Sky, Orcas, and Broncos. Division East featured Sin, Cheapskates, Earth, and Pengys. And Division West, for the third consecutive season, contained the HemoGoblins alongside SSS, Vanilla Thunder, and Yellow Fever.

The HemoGoblins had benefited from the West's weakness in both previous seasons. Nobody had found a way to exploit it against them. In the Michigan Games, with Sin quietly building something dominant in Division East, that structural advantage would matter far less than it had before.

Events 1–10

Sin Dominates Division East; the HemoGoblins Stumble

The divisional season of the Michigan Games was the most competitive in the Baldwin Ave era. The Orcas opened Event 1 with their customary maximum-15-point performance — a franchise tradition of brilliance at the wrong moment, this time on the way to a 12th-place finish. The Trinitarians won Event 2 with 15 points. And then Sin took over Division East.

Events 3 and 5 both belonged to Sin — back-to-back maximum-15-point performances that established them as the division's dominant force for the season. They added 14 points in Event 9, 11 in Event 2, and 10 in Event 8. Sin finished with 123 points, the highest total in the Michigan Games field and the highest final score of the entire Baldwin Ave era.

The Mowers matched them nearly point for point in Division North. Their divisional performances were rock-solid throughout — 13 in Events 1 and 4, double-digit scores in eight of ten events — and their final total of 121 would have been the championship score in either of the two previous seasons. In the Michigan Games, it was two points short.

The HemoGoblins' season was the most jarring storyline. They posted back-to-back maximums of 15 in Events 6 and 7 — two of the most dominant individual event performances in the field — but bookended them with a zero in Event 4 and scores of 3 in Events 2 and 9. It was their first ever divisional zero, the first time in three seasons they had looked genuinely vulnerable within a division. Their final total of 103.5 placed them sixth — their worst finish in MML history and a long fall from the back-to-back championships that had preceded it.

The HemoGoblins' First Zero

The HemoGoblins had never posted a zero in a divisional event across either of their championship seasons. In the Michigan Games they blanked in Event 4, then scored 3 in both Events 2 and 9 — three single-digit finishes in a single season against the same weak West Division that had fueled their back-to-back titles.

They still won Events 6 and 7 at maximum. They still posted 103.5 points. It was a genuinely good season by any other standard. But it was not good enough, and the dynasty — while not over — had been clearly interrupted for the first time.

Deep Sky produced the most electric individual stretch of the Michigan Games: maximum-15-point performances in both Events 8 and 9, the only back-to-back maximums in the field all season. Jeff's most frustrating franchise finished with 107 points and 5th place — their best finish since Season One. The drought reached its third year anyway.

The Champion

Sin: Joel's First Gold, Finally

Sin had done nothing in the first two seasons to suggest they were building toward a championship. Seventh in Season One. Eleventh in Season Two. The résumé was the definition of unremarkable. But the Michigan Games saw Sin put together the best complete-season performance in the Baldwin Ave era — consistent event-level excellence from start to finish, punctuated by two maximums and never a true collapse.

Their 123 final points edged the Mowers' 121 by two. The margin was narrow enough to feel like a near-miss for Jeff, and it was — but Sin had led the field in final score, and they earned it across the full ten events rather than in any single decisive moment. This was not a last-event drama like Season One. It was a season-long accumulation that simply couldn't be caught.

"Sin's 123 points was the highest final score in the history of the Baldwin Ave era. The Mowers' 121 would have won either of the two previous seasons."

Michigan Games · Final Standings

For Joel, it was a long time coming. Two seasons of watching Sam's HemoGoblins win back-to-back while his own best teams — the Angels, the Trinitarians — finished just off the podium. Sin's championship was the answer to all of it. Not a flashy, come-from-behind victory, but a patient, methodical accumulation of points that left no doubt about which team had been the best in the field.

Team Stories

The Narratives That Defined the Season

The Mowers: The Most Agonizing Runner-Up Yet

Season One: runner-up by one point, lost in the final event. Season Two: 15th place. Season Three: runner-up again, by two points, with a 121-point total that would have been champion in any other year. The Mowers are one of the MML's most tortured franchises, and the Michigan Games was the cruelest chapter yet — a genuinely dominant divisional season that ran into something even better. They led Division North comfortably, never collapsed, and still finished second. No team in MML history has been so consistently excellent and so consistently denied.

Cheapskates: A Redemption Arc Completes

Cheapskates had finished 14th in Season One and dead last in Season Two. The Michigan Games produced their best finish in MML history — third place, with 114 points. They built it on the back of their strongest three-event stretch in franchise history: 13, 14, and 14 points in consecutive events late in the season. It remains the ceiling of what the Cheapskates have ever been, and they found it in the final Baldwin Ave season when it mattered most.

Joel's Sweep of the Top Four

The Michigan Games final standings feature Sin (1st), Cheapskates (3rd), and Trinitarians (4th) — three of Joel's five teams occupying three of the top four spots. His fourth-place Trinitarians finished with 112 points. His champion Sin finished with 123. Joel placed teams in four of the top seven spots in the full sixteen-team field. It was the most dominant single-owner performance of the Baldwin Ave era, and it produced a championship from a team that had never previously sniffed the podium.

Deep Sky: Back-to-Back Maximums, Still No Title

Deep Sky posted 15 points in both Events 8 and 9 — the only consecutive maximum performances in the Michigan Games field. Their 5th-place finish at 107 points was their best since Season One. It was also, once again, not a championship. The drought reached three seasons. It would not break for a long time yet.

The Angels: Quietly Slipping

The Angels had been runners-up in Season Two. In Season Three they finished 8th with 95 points — a noticeable step backward. The team that had looked like a genuine rival to the HemoGoblins after the Colorado Classic appeared to have lost something. It wouldn't be the last time the Angels surprised everyone — in either direction.

The End of the Baldwin Ave Era

Three Seasons, Three Champions, One Street

The Michigan Games closed the book on the Baldwin Ave era — three seasons played in the same house on the same street where the MML was founded. Each season produced a different champion. Season One gave the league its dynasty. Season Two confirmed it. Season Three ended it, at least temporarily. After the Michigan Games, life had already scattered the founders to different states. Three years would pass before they reunited. When they came back, everything would be bigger. But the foundation — three friends, a bag of marbles, and a house on Baldwin Avenue — had been built to last.

What Came Next

Three Years of Silence, Then a Return

After the Michigan Games, the college era of the MML ended. The founders graduated, scattered to different states, and the league went quiet. The tally after three seasons: Sam had two titles, Joel had one, Jeff had none. Deep Sky had never won. The Trinitarians had never won. The Mowers had finished second twice and 15th once.

Three years later, in 2021, they came back. The league expanded from sixteen to twenty teams. A new venue — Joel's house in Michigan — hosted a new era. New formats, new faces, new rivalries. The New Age MML had arrived. And the first question it needed to answer was the one the Michigan Games had raised: was the HemoGoblins dynasty truly over, or merely taking a breath?
