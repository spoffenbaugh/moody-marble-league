MML Season Five

Season Five · Iowa · 2023

Mach 3

The league came to Iowa, expanded to 24 teams, and produced the most chaotic season in MML history. The reigning champions finished 17th. The Angels finally won. And Sin had one of the worst postseason performances any team has ever turned in.

24 Teams ◆ 4 Divisions of 6 ◆ 10 Events + 3-Round Rolloffs ◆ Sam's House · Iowa

Mach 3 was hosted by Sam in Iowa — a first for the MML, which had spent its entire existence in either Spokane or Michigan. The league expanded again, to 24 teams across four divisions of six. New franchises joined the field: the Twisters, Big Cats, Flower Power, and GotToGo arrived for their debut seasons alongside all the familiar faces. What followed was the most upending tournament in MML history — a season that scrambled every expectation and produced results that nobody in the room was prepared for.

The HemoGoblins finished 17th. The worst finish of their existence, by a margin that defies the previous contours of their career. A team that had never finished below 6th simply ran out of road in the regular season and never made the postseason. The Twisters — one of four brand new franchises — edged them out of a Rolloff berth in their own division. It was the defining shock of the season, and it reverberated through everything that followed.

Sin, coming off a championship in Season Three and a runner-up finish in Season Four, earned a first-round bye as their division winner. Then they scored zero, one, and zero across three Rolloff Round Two events and were eliminated with the worst postseason performance any team has ever recorded. The Angels, meanwhile, had quietly built the most complete campaign of their franchise history — and when the dust settled in the final round, they were champions for the first time.

The Format

A Three-Round Rolloff Unlike Anything Before

Mach 3 · Season Structure

Regular Season Into Rolloffs

01
Regular Season

24 teams across 4 divisions of 6. Placement-based scoring within each division across 10 events. Division winners earn a first-round bye.

02
Rolloff Round 1

8 non-bye teams compete in 3 events with scores reset. Top 4 advance. Bottom 4 eliminated.

03
Rolloff Round 2

4 bye teams + 4 Round 1 survivors. Scores reset. 3 events. Top 4 advance. Bottom 4 eliminated.

04
Super Roll II

Final 4 compete across 3 events. Higher score wins. Placements 1st through 4th decided.

The Regular Season

Pengys Lead the Field, but the Stories Are in the Middle

The regular season ran ten events with placement-based scoring within each division — teams earned points based on where they finished among the five other teams in their group. The division winner in each of the four divisions would receive a first-round bye. Everyone else who made the Rolloffs would have to earn it from the bottom up.

Pengys led the entire field with 34 points — their best regular season performance in MML history. Sin was second at 33. Trinitarians and Angels both finished at 32, splitting the remaining two bye spots. The four division winners headed into the Rolloffs without having to play Round One. For three of the four, the bye proved decisive. For one of them, it provided only a longer runway to disaster.

The eight teams that did not win their divisions but still accumulated enough points to reach Round One were Jawbreakers, Double Truffle, Purps, Expressos, Big Cats, Deep Sky, SSS, and Twisters. Twelve teams were eliminated entirely in the regular season — including the HemoGoblins, who finished with 23.02 points. The Twisters, in just their debut season, had edged them out for the final Rolloff berth in their shared division with a score of 24. The three-time champions had been eliminated before the postseason had even begun.

The Twisters Take the HemoGoblins' Spot

The HemoGoblins finished the Mach 3 regular season with 23.02 points — a total that reflects the tiebreaker decimal that couldn't save them. The Twisters, a brand new franchise in their very first MML season, finished their shared division with 24 points and claimed the Rolloff berth that the HemoGoblins needed.

For context: the HemoGoblins had never previously finished below 6th in five seasons. Their three championships represent more titles than any other franchise. In Mach 3, they were knocked out of the postseason in the regular season by a first-year team that went on to score just 1 point in Round One before being eliminated themselves. It was a historically bad finish made more painful by the manner of it.

New franchise Fracture had the worst regular season in the field, scoring just 12 points across ten events and finishing 24th — last. Flower Power and GotToGo both put up respectable debut totals for new teams at 22 and 25 respectively, though neither made the Rolloffs. The Sleep Apps — also a new team — finished 16th. Of the four new franchises, none made it past the regular season in their debut.

Rolloff Round One

Scores Reset — Eight Teams, Three Events, Four Survive

Eight teams entered Round One with scores wiped clean. Three events — Obstacle First Down is Out, Obstacle Last Down is Out, and Plinko to OC Combo — would determine which four advanced. The four that did would join the bye teams in Round Two. The four that didn't were done.

Jawbreakers and Double Truffle led the way with 15 points apiece — the joint highest in the round. Expressos and SSS both finished at 13, advancing as well. Deep Sky came agonizingly close at 12, one point short of advancing, in what would prove to be the end of another Deep Sky postseason run that fell just short of the final. Purps scored 11. Big Cats managed 4. And the Twisters — the team that had knocked the HemoGoblins out of the regular season — scored 1 point across three events and were eliminated immediately. Whatever they had done to edge out the dynasty in the regular season, they couldn't replicate it when the stakes were reset.

Rolloff Round Two

The Bye Teams Arrive — and Sin Collapses

Round Two brought the four bye teams — Pengys, Sin, Trinitarians, and Angels — into competition against the four Round One survivors. Scores reset again. Three events. Top four advance to the final. The bye teams carried the weight of expectation; they had earned their first-round exemption by leading their divisions across ten events. Three of the four justified it. The fourth produced the most stunning postseason collapse in MML history.

Pengys won Round Two outright with 16 points — the top score in the round. The Angels followed with 15. Trinitarians and Double Truffle both finished at 12, both advancing. That was four teams. The five, six, seven, and eight were Expressos (11), Jawbreakers (9), SSS (8), and Sin.

Sin scored 0, 1, 0 across three events. One point total. A team that had won the Michigan Games championship in Season Three, come within two points of back-to-back titles in Season Four, and earned a first-round bye as their division's best team in Season Five, played three Rolloff events and produced a combined single point. It is the worst postseason performance in MML history. The numbers are not a misprint. Zero. One. Zero.

"Sin scored zero, one, zero across three Rolloff Round Two events. A team that had been within two points of a back-to-back championship produced the worst postseason performance in MML history."

Mach 3 · Rolloff Round Two

What makes the result even harder to explain is that Sin had been excellent in the regular season — 33 points, second in the full field of 24, a dominant division winner. The collapse wasn't the product of a mediocre regular season catching up to them. It happened despite a strong regular season, in a format where scores reset and every team theoretically started level. Sin simply had three events that produced nothing, and finished the season in 8th place.

Super Roll II

The Final Four — Angels Take the Title

Four teams remained: Pengys, Angels, Trinitarians, and Double Truffle. Three events — a Plinko, a Triple Combo First Down is Out, and a Triple Combo Last Down is Out — would decide the championship. In this phase, higher scores win.

The Angels won the Super Roll II with 8 points — scores of 2, 3, and 3 across the three events. It was a measured, consistent final performance from a team that had built the season the same way: not the highest ceiling in the field, but the most reliable floor at the moments that mattered most. Pengys finished second with 6. Trinitarians took third with 3. Double Truffle finished fourth with 1.

For Joel, the Angels' championship was a long time coming. The franchise had been runners-up in Season Two, 8th in Season Three, eliminated in the regular season in Season Four, and had now finally won in Season Five. It was Joel's second championship gold — his first had come with Sin in Season Three — but the first ever for the Angels as a franchise. A team that had existed since the very first MML event in 2016, watched the HemoGoblins win twice, watched Sin win once, and finally earned their own moment on the top step in Iowa in 2023.

Team Stories

History Made, History Broken

The Trinitarians Finally Reach the Podium

The Trinitarians had been one of the MML's persistent near-miss franchises — 8th in Season One, 4th in Season Three, 5th in Season Four. The podium had been close but always out of reach. Mach 3 changed that. A 32-point regular season, a first-round bye as division champions, and a Round Two performance of 12 points got them to the final. Third place in the Super Roll II was the best finish in franchise history. It was also, for a team that had been around since the beginning and always seemed to be one result away, a genuinely significant moment.

Pengys: From 34 Regular Season Points to Runner-Up

Pengys led the entire field in the regular season with 34 points — the best regular season performance in the franchise's history and one of the strongest in the field all-time. They won Round Two with 16 points. They finished second in the final with 6. For a franchise that had spent much of the MML's history in the middle of the pack, runner-up at Mach 3 represented the ceiling of what they had ever achieved. Joel's team that finished closest to the Angels had also been one of the best regular season stories of the entire season.

Deep Sky: One Point from the Final

Deep Sky finished Round One with 12 points — one behind Expressos and SSS, one short of advancing. After seasons of near-misses in different formats, Jeff's franchise came within a single point of the final round of competition and was eliminated. Their 9th-place finish was respectable; the manner of it was not satisfying for a team whose talent had never been the issue. The championship drought extended to five seasons.

HemoGoblins: A Dynasty's Darkest Chapter

There is no way to soften what happened to the HemoGoblins in Mach 3. Three championships in five seasons. Never finished lower than 6th. And then, in Iowa, in a division with a brand new franchise, they accumulated 23 regular season points and were eliminated before the Rolloffs began. The Twisters — in their very first MML season — edged them by one point for the final Rolloff berth in Division D. The dynasty's worst finish is more than a data point. It is a reminder that no team, regardless of history, is immune to an off season. The HemoGoblins had theirs in 2023, and they had it at the worst possible time.

Historical Significance

The Season That Turned Everything Upside Down

Mach 3 produced more headline results than any previous season: the HemoGoblins' dynasty-worst 17th place; Sin's unfathomable 0-1-0 postseason collapse; the Trinitarians finally reaching the podium; the Angels winning their first franchise championship; and four new teams all making their debuts in the most expanded field the MML had ever seen. By the time the final marble settled in Iowa, the league's landscape looked almost unrecognizable. Whatever came next in the Iowa Open, it would inherit a more complicated, more uncertain MML than had ever existed before.

Looking Ahead

The Iowa Open Awaits

Mach 3 left the MML in a state of genuine uncertainty. The HemoGoblins had fallen to 17th. Sin had self-destructed. Jeff had never won a championship in five seasons. The Angels were champions for the first time. And the league was about to expand again — this time to 32 teams — for the Iowa Open, the biggest tournament in MML history.

Whatever had been settled in Iowa in 2023 would be unsettled all over again in 2025.
